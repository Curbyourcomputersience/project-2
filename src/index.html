    Back Boards

  • Used to move non-ambulatory patients that are concious or unconious

  • No longer used as spinal immobilization device

  • Best used for patient extrication and carrying to the ambulance

  • Patient should not be on the backboard no longer then 20 minutes

  • Backboards should be cleaned before being put back into service

  • All belts and straps must be secured before moving patient

  • One person at the feet and one person at the head at all times!!



    • Spinal motion restriction protocol

    Cervical neck collars are user to restrict movment for patients with suspected :

  • Head injuries.
  • Neck injuries.
  • Traumatic brain injuries.

    • patients with collars may either sit at an angle (semi fowlers) or lie down (supine) on the ambuslance stretcher.

    Observers or medical personel may be asked to hold C-Spine while the EMT asses the patient and applies the collar.

    To hold C-Spine observer position behind patient and hold head and spine inline.
